Illuvium Season 3 on Hold! 5 Fun Things You Can Do in the Meantime

Season 2 of Illuvium has ended, but Season 3 isn’t coming anytime soon. Find out why, what it means for players, and how to prepare while waiting.

YSeason 2 of Illuvium is officially over! Are you looking forward to Season 3?

Well, you might be waiting for a while, as Kieran Warwick posted this on the Illuvium Discord:

Yes, sadly, Season 3 is temporarily on hold. Of course, take note that you can soon claim your Season 2 rewards—the delay won’t affect it in any way. Just take note that there’s a vesting period that starts from the end of Season 2.

Now, if you're wondering why this hold of Season 3 was made and what it means for you, don't worry—we’ve got you covered.

Read on as we give you the details—plus some things you can do while waiting for the third season to commence!

Why Is Season 3 paused?

As mentioned in the screenshot, the Illuvium DAO and community council have made the call to put Season 3 on pause, and it’s not without reason.

Tournaments and Leaderboards Aren’t Ready Yet

One of the biggest reasons for the hold is that tournaments and leaderboards, key features that will add competitive depth to all game modes, aren’t quite finished. They’re planned to roll out by the end of October, and the team wants these systems to be fully functional before kicking off a new season.

If you think about it, launching a season without leaderboards or competitive tournaments would make the experience feel incomplete. They’re crucial for tracking your progress and comparing your skills to other players. Without them, it would be hard to gauge who’s dominating the game, and a lot of the competitive thrill would be missing.

Ensuring a Balanced Playing Field

Pausing Season 3 also gives the developers time to ensure everything is balanced across all game modes. The last thing anyone wants is for a new season to launch and be riddled with imbalances or exploits that could ruin the experience. This time will allow the team to fine-tune the gameplay, ensuring that when the leaderboards and tournaments finally arrive, they’re fair and fun for everyone.

What’s in it for you? Well, by waiting, you get a better, more polished experience. No one wants to grind through a season only to feel like it’s unfair or broken, right? Taking the time now means fewer headaches later, and a smoother, more competitive experience when things go live.

Building Excitement and Hype

Lastly, there’s the strategic side of things. By holding off Season 3 until tournaments and leaderboards are ready, the team is giving the community something to look forward to. It’s like charging up a big ability—you want to release it at the perfect time for maximum impact. When these features launch at the end of October, there’s going to be a ton of hype, and the competitive scene is going to feel fresh and exciting.

Why is this smart? Well, the anticipation of tournaments and competitive leaderboards will fuel discussions, build strategies, and have everyone gearing up to hit the ground running. If Season 3 dropped now without these features, that buzz might die down quickly, and the competitive scene wouldn’t be nearly as engaging.

What to Do While Waiting for Season 3

With Season 3 on hold, what’s a Ranger to do? There’s still plenty to keep you busy while we wait for the leaderboards and tournaments to roll out. Check these suggestions out:

Explore Overworld and Arena

Season 2 might be over, but the Overworld and Arena are still active. Now’s a great time to dive deeper into the game without the pressure of rankings or airdrops. Want to perfect your Illuvial hunting strategy in the Overworld? Go for it. There are still tons of Illuvials to capture, resources to gather, and biomes to explore.

The Arena is also a great place to practice your combat skills. Without the pressure of leaderboards, you can test new strategies, try different Illuvial combinations, and experiment with augments without worrying about your rank. When Season 3 arrives, you’ll be fully prepared to take on the competition.

Learn New Skills You Can Use For Illuvium

Why not join the ranks of Scoriox, Viper, or Nijafe and talk about Illuvium if you feel like you have something to share about the game? You can do this by learning something new like content creation, video editing, or even streaming. You can create Illuvium content that’s not only engaging but also builds your presence in the community.

Who knows, you might even become a go-to figure for tips and tricks once Season 3 drops!

Optimize Your Collection and Gear

This pause is your chance to prepare smartly. Review your Illuvials and gear—focus on who needs leveling up, fusing, or augmenting. Avoid wasting time grinding without rewards; instead, take stock of what’s missing and get ready for tournaments.

Need help figuring out your gear or what Illuvial you should search for? Make sure to check out our Forge Assistant and our Team Builder!

Try Out Other Games or Interests

It doesn’t have to be all Illuvium all the time! While the team works on bringing you the best version of Season 3, why not check out other games in the Web3 space or even pick up that hobby you’ve been putting off? Whether it’s catching up on your gaming backlog, diving into new projects, or spending time with friends, taking a break can be a great way to recharge before diving back into the Illuvium grind.

Sometimes stepping away from the game for a bit gives you a fresh perspective and helps you return with more energy and ideas. And hey, you never know—you might pick up an idea from a different game that you can suggest to the Illuvium team!

Get Involved in the Community

While waiting for Season 3, use this time to connect with the community. Join the discussions on Discord or Twitter/X, share your theories about upcoming features, or even offer your own tips and strategies. With everyone waiting for the next big update, the community will be buzzing with excitement, and you’ll have plenty of chances to engage with other players.

Plus, you might meet your Illuvium squad that can help you crush it in Season 3 when it finally drops!

Final Thoughts

While waiting for Season 3 might feel like a setback, it's a strategic move that will make the upcoming season even more exciting. With tournaments and leaderboards on the way, the competitive scene is going to level up, and this break ensures everything is balanced for when it kicks off.

In the meantime, use this opportunity to practice, optimize your collection, and connect with the community. When Season 3 arrives, you’ll be fully prepared to crush it.
